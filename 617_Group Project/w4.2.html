The Fire

Ma Huang

Identified Active Components

Ephedra is pungent and slightly bitter in flavors, warm in nature. Ephedra mainly contains alkaloids such as L-ephedrine and D-pseudoephedrine, and also volatile oils.


Pharmacological Actions

Ephedra is often used to treat respiratory, skin and urinary problems. Ephedra is an important ingredient for clearing wind and cold in the superficial portion of the body. For exterior syndromes, such as common colds and flu that present with chills, fever, headache and general soreness, ephedra along with cassia twig is often indicated such as the Ephedra Decoction . When individuals have a weakened immune system, it is proper to use with prepared aconite root. Ephedra can relieve joint or bone pain. The herb often works with herbs such as notopterygium root, pubescent angelica root, largehead atractylodes rhizome, cassia twig and angelica root to promote blood circulation as well as reduce swelling and inflammation of the regions. For decoction, 3-10g each time, a higher dose is indicated for conditions like general swelling, joint or bone pain. Crude ephedra is used for promote perspiration and urinary; processed ephedra is used for respiratory conditions.
