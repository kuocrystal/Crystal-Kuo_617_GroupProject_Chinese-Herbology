Head Meridian

Bai Shao Yao

Identified Active Components

White peony root is rich in monoterpene glycosides, such as paeoniflorin, oxy-paeoniflorin, benzoylpaooniflorin, albiflorin R1, paeoniflorigenone, galloylpaeoniflorin, (Z)-(1S,5R)-β-pinen-10-yl-β-vicianoside, lacioflorin, paeonilactones (A-C), and daucos-terol. The root also has hydrolyzed tannic acid like 1,2,3,6-tetra-O-galloyl-β-D-glucose, (+)-catechin and 1,2,3,4,6-penta-O-galloyl-β-D-glucose. It also contains β-siiosierol, terpenoids and 33 types of volatile oil like benzoic acid and paeonol. The root produced in Sichuan Province has an acidic substance that can inhibit staphylococcus aureus. In the Pharmacopoeia of People's Republic of China (2010 Edition) - Part I, the level of paeoniflorin should not less than 1.25%, as the standard quality of white peony root.


Pharmacological Actions

White peony root is often used to treat sallow complexion, irregular menses, menstrual pain, excessive menses, sweating problems, pain in the chest, stomach, abdomen, and limbs, and headache and dizziness. The root works with cassia twig, fresh ginger and Chinese dates to treat excess sweating that due to cold and wind weakened the superficial portion of the body; in case if the symptom is serious, aconite root should be added in the remedy too. For different sweating problems, the root can work with herbs like astragalus root, pseudostellaria root, black soybean coat, oyster shell, and blighted wheat.
